Finding an Internship

Online Resources

The University of Michigan provides a variety of online tools to help you discover internship opportunities:

  • Handshake: University of Michigan's primary platform for posting internships, connecting employers with students, and showcasing career events.
  • UMSI CareerLink: CareerLink provides focused listings for students in the School of Information, as well as resume books and resources.
    • View the UMSI CareerLink Student Toolkit
  • LinkedIn: Apply directly to opportunities that match your interest and highlight your professional achievements.

To be successful in your internship search, be proactive and start your search early. Tailor your applications to highlight relevant skills, projects, and experiences.

Career and Internship Fairs

Attending career fairs is a great way to meet employers, ask questions, and explore career options. The Career Development Office hosts multiple fairs each year, including:

  • UMSI Career & Internship Fair: Focused on tech, information science, and data science opportunities.
  • University-wide Career Fairs: Broad exposure to organizations across industries.

It is helpful to prepare a polished resume, practice an elevator pitch, and research companies beforehand to make a strong impression.

Interviews

Interview Prep

Preparing for interviews is a good way to gain confidence and can set you apart from other candidates. Key tips include:

  • Review common interview questions for your field. This can include both behavioral and technical questions. For technical practice, consider using tools like W3Schools or Leetcodeto practice.
  • Understand your company's mission, products, culture, and value.
  • Utilize the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to organize your thoughts and experiences and share their impact.

Mock Interviews

Mock interviews can be a great way to prepare for your internship or job experiences. Practicing with a friend, colleague, or family member can lower interview anxiety and set you up for success. Mock interviews can also give you constructive feedback on your communication and body language. To prepare for your interviews, consider utilizing some CDO resources:

    • Review our Intro to Interviewing Slides to learn the basics about job and internship interviews.
  • [Handout] Interview Protocol: Pre-Interview Worksheet
    • Use the Pre-Interview worksheet to prepare research about your target company, organize your work and educational experiences, and personal qualities.
  • [Handout] Interview Protocol: Questions to Ask in an Interview
    • CDO recommends preparing questions to ask in an interview. With this handout, learn about different question types and best practices.

Securing your Internship

Internship Credit

The School of Information allows students to earn up to 9 academic credits for internship experiences. Securing an internship is not only valuable experience, but can count toward your degree program. Speak with an advisor for guidelines and to learn about the credit registration process and internship course.

Best Practices

Starting Strong

Your first days set the tone for your internship. To start out strong, some strategies include:

  • Understanding your role and expectations.
  • Building relationships with your supervisor and team.
  • Setting goals to maximize your learning and professional growth.

Negotiating Job Offers

When you receive an offer, consider not just salary but also professional development, responsibilities, and company culture. The Career Development Office advisors can provide guidance on negotiation strategies to ensure a fair and positive outcome and wages.

Exiting your Internship

Ending your internship on a high note is just as important as staring strong. Some steps to take might include:

  • Seeking feedbacking from your supervisor.
  • Reflecting on skills and experiences gained.
  • Updating your resume, LinkedIn, and portfolio with accomplishments.
  • Maintaining connections with colleagues for future networking opportunities.
